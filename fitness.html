60K for 60 Years

Training for July 2026

My 60th birthday lands on July 7, 2026. Rather than accept the inevitable march of time with grace and dignity, I've decided to publicly commit to something sufficiently ridiculous to prove my stupidity levels are still operating at peak capacity: a 60-kilometer ultramarathon run the weekend before my birthday.

This isn't about inspiration or proving some grand philosophical point about aging. It's accountability—making a public commitment that I can't quietly abandon when training gets hard or weather gets terrible or my body reminds me that 60 years is, in fact, a long time. If I write it down with a timestamp and invite people to watch, future-me can't conveniently forget or rationalize backing out.

Below is my complete training plan with real-time updates after every run. Photos, progress notes, honest assessments of what's working and what definitely isn't. If you want to join me for a (very slow) training run or have thoughts on the plan, reach out. I'm updating this regularly as the training progresses.

15-Week Training Plan

Plan Overview

This is a structured 15-week program running from January through April, building toward a 60K race in early July. The plan progresses through distinct phases with clear objectives at each stage.

Weeks 1-3: Foundation Phase

Building base fitness with easy runs of 5-7km combined with consistent F45 resistance training (5 sessions per week). This phase establishes the conditioning foundation necessary for later intensity.

Week 4: Recovery Week

Reduced volume to allow adaptation and recovery before increasing training load. Critical for preventing injury and overtraining.

Weeks 5-7: Endurance Development

Introduction of tempo runs and transition to trail-specific terrain. Long runs begin extending beyond comfortable distances. This is where the real work starts.

Weeks 8-12: Peak Training Block

Long runs progressively increase to 32-40km. F45 sessions reduced to 3 times per week to accommodate increased running volume and recovery needs. Power-hiking practice on hills becomes essential. Race nutrition and hydration protocols get tested repeatedly.

Weeks 13-15: Final Build and Taper

Final long run of 40km in week 14, incorporating all race-day strategies. Practice with early starts or night-running. Then controlled taper to arrive at race day fresh but fit.

Weekly Structure

  • 2-3 Running Sessions: Mix of easy runs, tempo work, and progressive long runs
  • F45 Resistance Training: Integrated throughout the plan (5 sessions early weeks, reducing to 3 during peak training)
  • Progressive Long Runs: Building systematically from comfortable distances to 40km by week 14
  • Recovery Days: Structured rest and mobility work—not optional
  • Trail-Specific Training: Transitioning from road/flat surfaces to actual trail terrain and elevation
  • Race Simulation: Testing nutrition, hydration, gear, and pacing strategies during long runs
